What is Finance?
Finance is a term for matters regarding the management, creation, and study of money and investments. Specifically, it deals with the questions of how an individual, company or government acquires money – called capital in the context of a business – and how they spend or invest that money. Finance is then often split into the following major categories: corporate finance, personal finance and public
At the same time, and correspondingly, finance is about the overall "system" i.e., the financial markets that allow the flow of money, via investments and other financial instruments, between and within these areas; this "flow" is facilitated by the financial services sector. Finance therefore refers to the study of the securities markets, including derivatives, and the institutions that serve as intermediaries to those markets, thus enabling the flow of money through the economy.




 

Who Should Impart Financial Awareness?

Financial knowledge should be imparted to the students from a young age. At home, parents and families must allow their children to learn about it by providing them with opportunities to earn their pocket money, by helping in household chores etc. Further, schools should take steps to incorporate this important life skill as a part of their curriculum. Activities at schools should be so designed in a manner that all students irrespective of the stream of study (commerce, science or humanities) chosen by the them, should be aware enough to take sound financial decisions and be able to manage their own finances in the long-run.




 

What is the difference between FINANCIAL LITERARCY and FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT?

Financial literacy means “the ability to understand and effectively use various financial skills, including personal financial management, budgeting, and investing.” Whereas, financial management means “planning, organizing, directing and controlling the financial activities such as procurement and utilization of funds”. Benefits Of Financial Awareness- This awareness helps individual to make better financial decisions in many ways, some of them are: It provides one the knowledge to take better decisions on saving, investment, loans, insurance, budgeting, etc. Financial independence (particularly in old age), helps reducing the burden of debt. It equips an individual to reduce any financial stress especially, in difficult and uncertain times. Apart from individual finance related matters, financial awareness enables one to understand the reasons & impact of many policy decisions of the government (especially the one’s related to monetary and fiscal policies).

 

What is GST?

  • Goods and service tax(GST) is an Indirect Tax which has replaced many Indirect Taxes in India.
  • There are 3 taxes applicable under this system: CGST, SGST & IGST.
  • 1.CGST: Collected by the Central Government on an intra-state sale
  • 2.SGST: Collected by the State Government on an intra-state
  • 3.IGST: Collected by the Central Government for inter-state sale

    Difference between savings and investment

  • 1.Savings
  • Meaning-Surplus of income over expenditure
  • Nature-A natural occurence
  • Objective-Securing one's money
  • Outcome-Savings do not yeild huge returns
  • 2.Investment
  • Meaning-Sacrifice of current consumption for future benefit
  • Nature-A conscious activity
  • Objective-Giving one's money the potential to grow over time
  • Outcome-Investments have the potential to yeild huge returns











    • What is Asset allocation and risk tolerance?

  • Asset allocation is an investment stratergy that attempts to balance risk versus reward(i.e. maximizing returns and minimizing risk) by adjusting the percentage of each asset in an investment portfolio.
  • Asset allocation helps in risk reduction
  • Asset allocation primarily depends on the risk tolerance of an investor
  • Risk tolerance means the degree of variability in investment returns that an individual investor is willing to withstand.
